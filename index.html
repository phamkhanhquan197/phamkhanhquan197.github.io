About Me

Welcome to my homepage!!! ^^ I am Pham Khanh Quan, a Ph.D. student in Information and Communication Engineering at Chungbuk National University, South Korea, and a member of NCLab. I received my M.S. degree in the same field from Chungbuk National University in 2023, and my B.S. in Electronics and Telecommunications Engineering from Ton Duc Thang University, Vietnam in 2020. My academic journey has included extensive research in federated learning, edge AI, and deep learning systems. I have worked on practical applications such as container orchestration, reinforcement learning for precision laser cutting, and deployment of large models in resource-constrained environments. My core interests lie in advancing intelligent and adaptive computing solutions for real-world, decentralized systems.

Research Interests

My research focuses on efficient and adaptive optimization for federated learning, with an emphasis on addressing data heterogeneity and communication efficiency. I am currently working on singular value decomposition (SVD)-based low-rank adaptation techniques, such as FedKLS, which leverage KL-divergence to dynamically select singular components tailored to client distributions. My broader interests include personalized and communication-efficient FL, and the integration of lightweight model adaptation methods (e.g., LoRA variants) for domain generalization and on-device intelligence. In addition, I apply reinforcement learning to automate hyperparameter tuning and decision-making processes in intelligent systems, especially in the context of robotics and manufacturing optimization.
